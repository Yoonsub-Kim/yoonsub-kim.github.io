Bio

I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. at the Department of Environmental Sciences and Engineering (ESE) at the University of North Carolina Gillings School of Global Public Health, where I am advised by Prof. Barbara Turpin. Before joining the Ph.D. program, I obtained my M.S. and B.S. at the Korea University, advised by Prof. Jung-Hwan Kwon. During my Master's program, I studied on the fate of hazardous chemicals in the environment, and I interned at the Korea Testing Laboratory (KTL), where I analyzed environmental pollutants. Following master's degree, I conducted research at Samsung Electronics EHS/Infra Technology Research Center, focusing on the chemical supply system.

My current research explores the sampling and analysis of PFAS in the atmosphere, with a specific focus on understanding their environmental behavior. This includes examining how PFAS are emitted and transported in ambient air. I am particularly interested in quantifying emission rates from a range of consumer and industrial products, as this information is essential for identifying major sources and assessing potential human exposure risks.
